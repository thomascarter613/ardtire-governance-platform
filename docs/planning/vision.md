# Project Vision

## Statement

The Ardtire Governance Platform is the institutional operating platform for the Ardtire Society.
It exists to support formal governance processes with auditable, rule-driven workflows for
membership, proposals, deliberation, voting, ratification, publication, and records.
The platform prioritizes correctness, legitimacy, transparency, accessibility, and long-term
institutional durability over superficial speed or demo polish.

## Strategic Purpose

The platform serves two simultaneous institutional registers:

1. **Public Philosophical Society** — An open membership community engaged in civic and
   philosophical inquiry, with transparent governance and published deliberation records.

2. **Protected Kingdom Institution** — The interior institutional layer of the Kingdom of Ardtire,
   with access controls, ceremony, and formality appropriate to constitutional governance.

The platform enforces the boundary between these registers at every layer — identity, permission,
data access, and publication.

## Design Principles

- **Correctness before convenience** — A slower, accurate governance process is preferable to a
  fast, incorrect one.
- **Auditability as infrastructure** — The audit log is not an add-on. It is a first-class citizen
  of the data model.
- **Documentation as a first-class artifact** — Undocumented decisions do not exist.
- **Operational humility** — The platform is operated by one person. Complexity must earn its place.
- **Institutional durability** — The platform must outlast any individual technology trend.

## Success Criteria

- A member can navigate the full proposal → deliberation → vote → ratification lifecycle
  without requiring direct operator intervention.
- Every consequential governance action is recorded with sufficient fidelity to reconstruct
  institutional history from the audit log alone.
- The platform can be restored from backup within the RTO target (8 hours) by a single operator.
- Ratified records are published in a form suitable for treatment as official institutional documents.
